---

# HOW THEY FIT TOGETHER

These frameworks complement each other by addressing different layers of governance, risk, compliance, and operational security.

### COBIT 2019 provides the governance umbrella.

It defines:

- governance structures,
- accountability,
- decision-making processes,
- performance measurement,
- and strategic alignment between IT and business objectives.

### ISO 27001 provides the formal security management system (ISMS).

It establishes:

- security policies,
- risk treatment processes,
- security controls,
- audits,
- documentation,
- and continuous improvement mechanisms required for certification.

### NIST CSF 2.0 provides operational cybersecurity guidance.

It supports the implementation of practical cybersecurity capabilities across:

- governance,
- identification,
- protection,
- detection,
- response,
- and recovery functions.

### COSO strengthens enterprise risk management and internal controls.

It ensures that:

- business risks,
- operational risks,
- compliance risks,
- and financial risks

are managed consistently at the executive and board levels.

Together, these frameworks create a layered governance model that enables VaultEdge to:

- improve governance maturity,
- achieve ISO 27001 certification,
- strengthen cybersecurity resilience,
- satisfy regulatory and customer requirements,
- and support sustainable business growth.

# Why These 4 Frameworks Are the Best Combination for VaultEdge

This combination specifically addresses VaultEdge’s biggest problems identified in the case study:

| VaultEdge Problem                            | Framework That Helps Most |
| -------------------------------------------- | ------------------------- |
| No governance structure                      | COBIT 2019                |
