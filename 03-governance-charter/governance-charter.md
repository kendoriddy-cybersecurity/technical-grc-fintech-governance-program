# 1. PURPOSE

This Information Security & IT Governance Charter establishes the formal governance structure, responsibilities, and oversight mechanisms required to protect VaultEdge Financial Technologies’ information assets, technology infrastructure, customer data, and business operations.

The governance program exists to ensure that information security, risk management, and compliance activities are aligned with VaultEdge’s business objectives, regulatory obligations, customer expectations, and investor requirements.

This charter establishes clear accountability for security and governance decisions and ensures that risks are identified, assessed, managed, monitored, and reported appropriately across all business operations.

The program also supports VaultEdge’s objective of achieving ISO 27001 certification, improving operational resilience, strengthening customer trust, and maintaining compliance with applicable regulatory and contractual requirements.

# 2. SCOPE

This governance program applies to:

### People:

All employees, executives, contractors, consultants, interns, third-party service providers, and partner organizations with access to VaultEdge systems, applications, networks, or data.

### Locations:

All VaultEdge operational locations including:

- London, United Kingdom (Headquarters)
- Berlin, Germany
- Singapore
- Nairobi, Kenya
- Remote employees and contractors (USA and globally)

### Systems:

All information systems, cloud infrastructure, applications, databases, endpoints, development environments, CI/CD pipelines, collaboration tools, and third-party integrated systems including AWS, Google Cloud, GitHub, Jira, Slack, Jenkins, PostgreSQL, MongoDB, Datadog, Snowflake, and related platforms.

### Products:

- VaultPay
- VaultWallet
- VaultConnect API
- All supporting internal platforms and services

### Data:

All customer, employee, financial, operational, payment, authentication, business, and regulatory data processed, stored, transmitted, or managed by VaultEdge, including personally identifiable information (PII), payment card data, and confidential business information.

### Third parties:

All vendors, suppliers, contractors, cloud providers, payment processors, software providers, integration partners, and external organizations that store, process, transmit, or access VaultEdge information assets or systems.

# 3.1 GOVERNANCE BODIES

This program is overseen through the following formally established governance bodies, as defined in the Governance Organizational Structure document (Activity 3):

### Board-level committees:

- **Board Risk & Security Committee** — cybersecurity risk oversight, governance program oversight, major incident review, risk appetite approval, quarterly security reporting review, and ISO 27001 readiness oversight
- **Audit & Compliance Committee** — regulatory compliance oversight, internal audit coordination, PCI-DSS and GDPR compliance monitoring, and audit findings remediation review
- **Technology & Innovation Committee** — technology strategy oversight, cloud governance, major technology investments, technology risk evaluation, and alignment between engineering and business goals

### Executive-level committee:

- **IT Steering Committee** — cross-functional alignment of business priorities, security investments, compliance constraints, and product engineering timelines (chaired by the CISO; meets monthly)

These bodies replace prior informal coordination practices (such as unstructured weekly alignment calls among the CTO, CFO, and VP of Engineering) and constitute the authoritative governance structure for VaultEdge.

# 4. OBJECTIVES

a. Establish a formal information security and IT governance structure with clearly defined accountability and reporting lines.

b. Achieve ISO 27001 certification within 12 months.

c. Develop and maintain an enterprise-wide risk management program covering operational, cybersecurity, compliance, and third-party risks.

d. Ensure compliance with applicable regulatory and contractual obligations including GDPR, PCI-DSS, UK FCA regulations, Kenya Data Protection Act, and Singapore PDPA.

e. Reduce the likelihood and impact of cybersecurity incidents through the implementation of appropriate controls, monitoring, awareness training, and incident response capabilities.

f. Improve visibility, oversight, and governance reporting through defined KPIs, board reporting mechanisms, and the governance bodies established in Section 3.1 (Board Risk & Security Committee, Audit & Compliance Committee, Technology & Innovation Committee, and IT Steering Committee).

g. Strengthen customer trust, investor confidence, and operational resilience by implementing sustainable governance, risk, and compliance practices aligned with industry standards.

---

# 5. GOVERNANCE PRINCIPLES

This program is guided by:

a. **Accountability** — Security, risk, and compliance responsibilities shall be clearly assigned, communicated, and enforced across the organization.

b. **Strategic Alignment** — IT and security activities shall support VaultEdge’s business objectives, growth strategy, and regulatory obligations.

c. **Risk-Based Decision Making** — Decisions regarding technology, security, and compliance shall be guided by risk assessments and defined risk appetite levels.

d. **Continuous Improvement** — Governance processes, controls, and security capabilities shall be regularly reviewed, measured, and improved.

e. **Transparency & Oversight** — Governance reporting, incident escalation, and compliance monitoring shall provide timely and accurate visibility to executive management and the board.

---

# 6. REVIEW CYCLE

### Review frequency:

This charter shall be reviewed annually or upon significant organizational, regulatory, operational, or security changes.

### Triggers for early review:

- Major cybersecurity incidents or data breaches
- Significant regulatory or legal changes
- Organizational restructuring or acquisition
- Material technology changes or cloud migrations
- Audit findings or certification gaps
- Board or executive management request
