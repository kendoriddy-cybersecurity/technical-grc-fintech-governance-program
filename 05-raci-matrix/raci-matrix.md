# Short Justification for Key Assignments

## Why the Board is Accountable for Risk Appetite

Risk appetite is a strategic business decision affecting revenue, compliance exposure, investor confidence, and operational risk. Final accountability therefore sits with the Board.

---

## Why the CISO Owns Most Security & Governance Activities

The CISO is the executive responsible for:

- security governance,
- enterprise security strategy,
- incident management,
- compliance oversight,
- and board reporting.

This aligns with the governance structure established earlier.

---

## Why the DPO Is Accountable for Data Protection

Under GDPR and related privacy laws, the Data Protection Officer (DPO) has direct accountability for privacy governance, regulatory coordination, and data protection compliance.

---

## Why the CTO Is Accountable for Change Management

Technology changes are operational engineering functions involving infrastructure, deployment pipelines, application releases, and production systems. Therefore, accountability rests with the CTO, while security and GRC provide oversight and consultation.

---

# Governance Logic Behind the Matrix

This RACI structure intentionally:

- separates governance oversight from operational execution,
- avoids concentration of power,
- supports the Three Lines Model,
- strengthens accountability,
- and aligns with ISO 27001 governance expectations.

It also ensures that:

- the Board provides oversight,
- executives provide strategic direction,
- the CISO governs security,
- the GRC team executes governance processes,
- and engineering maintains operational responsibility for technology implementation.
